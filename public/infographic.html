Procedural Roadmap

Anticipating the Defendant's steps allows us to prepare for the likely path of this litigation.

Step 1: Notice to Defend

Defendant formally indicates their intention to oppose the action.

Step 2: Special Plea (Jurisdiction)

Defendant will likely argue the case should be heard in Cape Town. This is their strongest preliminary move.

Outcome A: Plea Dismissed

The Court agrees jurisdiction is proper. The case proceeds on the merits in Gqeberha. High probability.

Outcome B: Plea Upheld

We must re-issue summons in the Western Cape. This incurs costs and delays but does not end the claim. Low probability.

Step 3: Plea on the Merits

Defendant will file their formal defence, denying the agreement, performance, and quantum.

Step 4: Discovery & Trial

Both parties exchange evidence. The matter proceeds to trial where witness credibility and documentary evidence will be tested.

Path Forward

Issue summons and serve on both the Defendant's registered address and the Motherwell address. Plaintiff must collate all possible evidence to support the claim.
